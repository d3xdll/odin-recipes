  • Cook Time: 1 hour 30 mins
  • Servings: 6
  • Difficulty: easy

Ingridients

For cheesecake base:

  • 800g full-fat cottage cheese

  • 2 medium-sized potatoes

  • 50g room-temperature unsalted butter

  • 180-200g granulated sugar

  • 4 eggs

  • 1 lemon

  • 1 orange

  • Vanilla sugar

  • 1 tbsp flour (plus about 1 tbsp to coat the baking dish)

  • 50g raisins (optional)

  • Salt

For salted caramel:

  • 150ml heavy cream

  • 150g granulated sugar

  • Salt

Here comes step that you should follow, because of the implication

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the cottage cream, granulated sugar, eggs,
    flour, a tsp vanilla sugar and a pinch of salt. Using a box grater,
    grate the zest of one lemon and orange and add to the cottage cheese mixture.
    Add the softened butter.

  2. Using a hand blender, blitz the cottage cheese mixture until it resembles a smooth shiny cream.
    If you like, you can mix in the raisins at this point.

  3. Boil the potatoes, skin on, and let them cook completely. Remove the skins and finely shred the potatoes using a box grater.

  4. Preheat the oven to 180C. Using a rubber spatula, carefully fold in the shredded potato into the cottage cheese mixture and stir until well-combined. Coat an ovenproof baking dish with butter and dust with about a tablespoon of flour. Pour the cottage cheese mixture into the prepared dish and level out with a spatula. Bake for about 60 minutes.

  5. While the cheesecake is baking, prepare the caramel sauce. In a small pan over low heat, melt the sugar with a pinch of salt. Resist the temptation to mix the sugar with a spatula - this will lead to crystallization and you'll end up with grainy caramel. You can, however, tilt the pan from side to side and gently swirl the melting sugar. Drizzle in the cream.

  6. Whisk the cream and melted sugar mixture until well-combined and smooth.

  7. Serve the cheesecake when it has cooled. Top with a drizzle of warm caramel sauce.

And here we have, a glorius dessert!
